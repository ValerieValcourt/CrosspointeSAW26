The Faithful's Mission

You carry the weight of the castle

⚜ Your Role

You are a Faithful. You know only your own loyalty. Among your 65 colleagues, 9 are secretly Traitors — and one Faithful is a Double Agent who holds classified knowledge about one Traitor's identity. You don't know which colleague is the Double Agent, but they are on your side.

Work together. Earn gold coins. Observe everything. At each of the four daily Roundtables (Tuesday through Friday), vote to banish the people you suspect. Root out every Traitor before Friday's Final Roundtable — or the Traitors win.

✦ How to Win

Banish all 9 Traitors across the four Roundtables (Tue–Fri). If every Traitor is gone by Friday's Final Roundtable — the Faithfuls win and split the glory. If even one Traitor survives to the end — the Traitors claim everything. The Fallen Explorers Council (eliminated players) casts one collective vote at the Final Roundtable, so no one is truly out of the game.

The Five Days of Fate

Click each day to reveal what's happening

I

Monday · Day One

The Gathering

"The game begins. The castle opens its doors."

▼

⚜ Dress Code

All black today. You are all Explorers... for now.

Morning
Opening

The Envelope Ceremony

All 65 staff open sealed role envelopes simultaneously in the cafetorium. Your card tells you whether you are a Faithful, a Traitor, or the Double Agent. You are a Faithful — loyal to the castle. Hold that loyalty and start watching.

Midday
Mission 1

Castle Build-Off

Teams compete to build the tallest, most creative structure using only index cards, tape, straws, and foil. 10 minutes. Three prizes: Tallest (+5 coins), Best Design (+3 coins, crowd vote), Most Dramatic Collapse (+2 coins). Compete hard. Build credibility. And start watching how people behave under pressure.

Afternoon
Mission 2

Staff Olympics — 3 Events

Cartographer's Draw: Blindfolded relay castle drawing — each person adds one element to a team drawing of Crosspointe Castle. Explorer's Sprint: Silly movement relay (crab walk, hop, backward walk, bear crawl). Cipher Toss: School trivia + ball catch — wrong answer and you sit down. Each event has coin prizes for the winning team. Watch for Traitors who are suspiciously good at rallying their side.

🗡 What the Traitors Are Doing Tonight

After you leave for the day, the 9 Traitors are quietly voting to eliminate one Faithful. A sealed black scroll will be placed on someone's desk overnight. You won't know until tomorrow morning.

II

Tuesday · Day Two

The Missions + Roundtable #1

"The first scroll is read. The first vote is cast."

▼
Morning
Scroll #1

First Elimination Revealed

The Game Master reads the first Black Scroll aloud at the morning gathering. One Explorer has been eliminated by the Traitors overnight. They receive a cape and join the Fallen Explorers Council. They will still have a vote at the Final Roundtable — their voice counts.

Midday
Mission 3

The Great Explorer Bake-Off

Each team gets a bag of random snacks and 15 minutes to create a "dish" — no baking, just assembly and presentation. Name it dramatically. Present in 2 minutes. Crowd votes by applause. Use this time to observe how your colleagues behave in a low-stakes group setting.

Afternoon
Mission 4

The Cipher Challenge

Teams race through a 5-clue campus escape-room hunt. The trail winds through the Mailroom, the Cafetorium, the Playground, and the Principal's Office before ending in a coded final message to decode. First team back with the answer wins 5 coins each. Watch how Traitors navigate this — they'll try to appear helpful while quietly sizing up the competition.

Late PM
Roundtable #1

First Vote — 60 Seconds Each

Everyone gets 60 seconds to speak, accuse, defend, or stay silent. Then you write one name on your ballot and place it in the Vote Urn. The person with the most votes is banished — and their role is revealed. Did you find a Traitor? Or did you cast out a Faithful? The most dramatic speech earns +2 bonus coins (crowd vote).

🗡 Tonight's Threat

After the Roundtable, the remaining Traitors choose their next elimination target. Another scroll will be delivered overnight.

III

Wednesday · Day Three

The Double Strike

"Two scrolls. One day. Watch for something strange."

▼

⚡ Two Scrolls Today

Wednesday is the most dramatic day of the week. You will receive two elimination revelations — one in the morning gathering, and one discovered when a scroll is found in plain sight in a common area around midday. The Traitors placed it there deliberately. This is a message: they are bold. They are not afraid of you.

Morning
Scroll #2

Second Elimination Revealed

The Game Master reads the scroll at the morning gathering. Cape, ceremony, and the Fallen Explorer joins the Council. Use this moment to observe who seems relieved, who seems surprised, and who seems too neutral.

~11am
Scroll #3 — In Plain Sight

The Public Scroll

A sealed scroll will appear somewhere visible in the building — on the lounge table, the coffee counter, a hallway ledge. When it's found and an emergency gathering is called, the eliminated person's name is inside. Watch the Traitors' faces when this is announced. Their reactions in the moment of genuine surprise are rare opportunities for Faithfuls. Also: be watchful of anyone who seems to be engineering unusual situations around you today — the Traitors have a challenge.

Midday
Mission 5

Among Us Lunch + Suspicion Dossiers

Every table receives a Suspicion Dossier — anonymous behavioral observations from the Game Master. A Confession Booth is available: a private curtained corner where you can share information with the Game Master. Use it if you have intel you don't want to share publicly. The GM will keep your identity protected.

Afternoon
Mission 6

The Artifact Hunt

12 artifacts hidden across campus with coin values (1, 2, or 5 coins). Teams race to collect the highest total in 30 minutes. Warning: some artifacts may have been "cursed" by the Traitors — they subtract 2 coins instead of adding. You won't know which ones until scoring. Watch who seems to know which artifacts to avoid.

Late PM
Roundtable #2

60 Seconds Each · Vote Carefully

Two eliminations already today. Emotions are high. The Traitors will use this energy to misdirect you. Stay focused on behavior patterns, not just speeches. Remember: the most passionate accuser is sometimes a Traitor deflecting. The quietest person in the room might be the most dangerous.

IV

Thursday · Day Four

The Betrayal

"The Double Agent awakens. A Traitor may try to recruit you."

▼
Morning
Scroll #4

The Evening Scroll Found

One Explorer arrived to find a scroll in their mailbox — placed there last night after school. They carried it home without knowing. This is the Traitors being methodical. Reveal at morning gathering.

Morning
Double Agent

The Double Agent Activates

The Game Master will publicly announce that the Double Agent — one of your Faithful colleagues holding classified Traitor intel — has until 3pm today to act on that information. They may reveal a Traitor publicly, or use the knowledge at the Roundtable. If no action by 3pm, the card expires. Watch who reacts unusually to this announcement. The Double Agent knows something. So do the Traitors.

✦ Recruitment Warning — Thursday Only

Today, one Traitor may approach you privately with a Recruitment Scroll — an invitation to join the Traitors. If this happens to you, you have two choices: (A) Accept and join the dark side, or (B) Refuse and keep the scroll as evidence to use at the Roundtable. Either choice is legitimate — but if you accept, you become a Traitor for the Final Roundtable. Choose carefully.

Midday
Mission 7

The Traitor's Feast + Black Chocolates

A full appreciation treat spread — but hidden inside are 3 chocolates wrapped in black foil. Each contains a clue card. When the GM announces this, actively look for the black chocolates. The clues may name a real Traitor, point to a real team, or be a red herring. Read them carefully. Share intel with trusted colleagues.

Afternoon
Mission 8

Coworker Trivia

During the appreciation time: 20 rounds of trivia about your colleagues, based on fun facts they submitted beforehand. 1 gold coin per correct answer. The highest scorer earns the Explorer's Compass badge.

Late PM
Roundtable #3

60 Seconds Each · One Day Left

You may have Double Agent intel surfacing today. The Traitors know the end is near and will be most deceptive in this vote. If the Double Agent reveals a Traitor — that person can be banished immediately or used as information for your vote strategy. One day remains after this.

End of Day

Wall of Explorers Unveiled

In the staff lounge — a portrait and anonymous compliment card for every single Explorer in the castle. Read yours. This is real. This is who your colleagues see when they look at you. The game is drama. This is truth.

V

Friday · Day Five

The Grand Finale

"Everything is decided today. Make your vote count."

▼
Morning
Scroll #5

Final Elimination Revealed

The Game Master reads the last scroll. The Final Dossier — a summary of the week's key clues and notable plays — is posted in the staff lounge. Review it before the Finale. Everything you've seen this week is now compiled in one place.

Midday
Grand Banquet

The Explorer's Grand Feast

The biggest, most celebratory lunch of the week. The Fallen Explorers Council returns to full status for the feast. Enjoy it. Strategize. Prepare your Final Roundtable speech.

Early PM
Roundtable #4 — THE FINAL VOTE

The Grand Finale

The Fallen Explorers Council casts one collective ballot. All surviving players vote. 60 seconds per person for final speeches — make yours count. The banished player reveals their role. Then: all remaining players stand. Traitors sit down. The truth surfaces. If no Traitors remain standing — Faithfuls win.

Finale
Awards

The Grand Awards Ceremony

8 awards presented by category. Then — every single Explorer is recognized by name with one specific, genuine thing they contributed this year. The game was fiction. The appreciation is real.

The Roundtable Faithful's Guide

Four votes. Four chances to find them.

⚜ The 60-Second Rule

Each player gets exactly 60 seconds to speak before each vote. Use yours. Don't waste it on general feelings — be specific. Name who you suspect and why. The most compelling speech earns bonus gold coins (crowd applause vote).

BehaviorWhat It Might Mean
